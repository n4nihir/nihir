1.Red Rose School, Deoghar

The beginning...

Red Rose is one of the prestigious schools of Deoghar, Jharkhand. Everytime I write Deoghar, I need to put Jharkhad with it as many of the folks don't know about Deoghar. With a brief intro, Deoghar is a religious place where one the famous Rawaneshwaram Temple was established by Raawan. Keeping these things apart, Red Rose shaped my dreams from class LKG to class 10th. Frankly speaking, I didn't like Red Rose much and even now I don't. I had always wanted to be in Saint Francis School. But due to some reasons, I couldn't have been there. Overall, Red Rose was a good enough experience for me and a scary one too. Teachers were like I'll grill you and eat if you commit a mistake. Somewhere between tortures regarding tuitions, Madam ka sardard, Khassi ka bompas town, Stadium ki Cpanti and all these shits, I grew up from a lean and thin baby (yeah baby, I was a baby when I was entering Red Rose on a red auto driven by Abhay Bhaiya) to a young lad with some shorts of hairy chin. One thing of Red Rose is very famous, The R.N. Singh. I'm not gonna talk about him as it scares me even now.

Red Rose was a great experience (excluding studies) and a greater one even if you have folks like Aakash, Vivek, Ranjan, Deepak, Manikant, Sudhanshu, Tanay, Raj and Sumit; with competitors like Kunal and Sneha; and obviously with girls like Shreyashi (I still remember the charm on her face). Teachers like Tarun Sir, Kiran Ma'am, KKP sir, Ramesh Sir and most importantly Ram Sewak Singh 'Gunjan' mended me.

Red Rose focussed on studying and only studying. No games, no extra-academics, no excursions, no everything except studying. The typical fucking bookish student. And sometimes I even guess why they call it a co-ed school. I won't suggest anyone about Red Rose until and unless he/she is unable to get a seat in Saint Francis and R.K. Mission and you are Nihir and there is a creation in Red Rose.

2.Chinmaya Vidyalaya, B.S. City

The nurture...

Chinmaya Vidyalaya is one of the most prestigious educational institutons not only in the state of Jharkhand but also for the nation. It's run by Chinmaya Mission headed currently by Swami Tejomayananda. Swami Tejomayananda is also awarded with a Padma Bhushan for his works in the field of education. As a proud 'Chinmayite', I would highlight the achievements of CV that it was placed as the number one school all over India by different surveying bodies. It's also awarded with the British Council Internationl School Award. You know, the NUMERO UNO. One of the finest creations of God, Dr. Ashok Singh, is the principal of this temple and he is accompanied by his team of very well qualified and efficient faculties. Some of them are research scholars from German Universities and some are IITians too. Being the NUMERO UNO, Chinmaya is a great place to learn and earn skills. Skills which lead us to a human being. It's not just passing the examination with A grade, it's about giving more than what we take, it's about thinking of 'Vasudhaiva Kutumbakam', it's about thinking for the mankind and spreading light with love. Although, I can't gather it all. Here I knew what co-ed is, it's about building self-confidence and performing better (as mentioned in the Physical Education book). I won't state anything about competitors as there was a tough competition there. Taking it to friends, Vaibhav, Aaquib, Aditya, Anish, Aditi, Prakriti and many more. O yeah, how can I leave it, there were also the beautiful young ladies Nikita, Varsha and Prakriti (Yes I did hit on her).

Coming to the workload, +2 is pretty much tougher, especially when you are also preparing for JEE. Things were sometimes like "You know I am a JEE aspirant and I can solve this question in just two lines which you may end up in 2 pages". These two things are different, one requires efficiency and the other requires labour. Attending those boring lectures of English in the morning, getting very very poor marks, relying on your own (as I was staying in a lodge away from my family in Deoghar) made me.

CV is also one of the most caring institutions for students encouraging them for Extra-Academics too and for academics, they also have their special doubt clearance cell available online. You can get a good company of people. Personally, I'll always suggest CV for someone because I have felt it. The leap from Red Rose to the NUMERO UNO CV feels like dreams. I still remember the motto 'Keep Smiling'.

3.IITian's PACE

Preparation for JEE...

IITian's PACE is a coaching institute in Bokaro which prepares students for IIT-JEE and different olympiads. Here, I was overburdened with the tests; the fortnightly ones, the major tests, the AITS, the practice test and many more. Thanks to Surya Prakash as because of him only I got a worthy seat in PACE. That was the perfect platform for JEE preparation for me. The very well qualified faculty team over there was very helpful. I still remember the study materials, the class notes, the boring lectures, the cut-throat competition over there and everything. Spending 4 hours from 16:00 to 20:00 over was like watering me everyday. PACE or more precisely, IITian's PACE is the best coaching institute in Bokaro for a newcomer.

4.IIT Kharagpur

The fun begins...

Indian Institute of Technology, Kharagpur is one of the most prestigious engineering institutions across the globe. IIT KGP is the mother of all IITs as it is the oldest one and the largest one too. Being an IITian is a great feeling in itself. But, the thing which is the worst about IIT KGP is the poor sex ratio. IIT KGP provides ample opportunities for career development. It provides you with thousands of other IITians too. It also has a great alumni network. The day is full of academic load as well as extra-academic load too. The busy schedule of classes exhausts us every day. Still we are suffering from the boring lectures. And the third grade torture of Engineering Drawing and Electrical Technology labs too along with the poor grades associated with them scares the shit out of us.

Apart from the workload, KGP is a damn good place. Being in college is a thing but, being in KGP is an emotion. You get to see talented folks around you and learn from them. Students are here from different parts of our vast and diverse motherland. You can improve upon your hobbies, your passions and many more things. Words are not enough to praise KGP. Till now, I've participated in KHOJ, Robosoccer, War of Wits, Funtoosh, water rocketry, NSS camp and much more to go. There is a high swag level in wearing IIT t-shirts and hoodies off-campus. I've also heard of people saying "IIT Kharagpur" with some exclamation while roaming in the streets of Deoghar. Ooo yeaaa, I'm lovin' it.
